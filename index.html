Roll through three scientific methods built to intercept urban surface water runoff.

Absorption Basin

Rain Garden Systems

Shallow planted basins engineered with deep organic soils, porous sand blends, and indigenous flora. These basins act like sponges, slowing high-velocity surface water, catching particulate trash, and absorbing nitrogen/phosphates directly into plant roots before runoff rejoins groundwater systems.

Riparian Armour

Green Vegetation Buffers

Dense vegetative rows consisting of thick grasses, deep-root shrubs, and water-loving trees aligned parallel to creeks and channels. Green buffers physically trap sediments, slow flow speed, and lock banks together to prevent erosion during flash floods.

Structural Filter

Sewer Filter Box

Compact concrete in-drain containers engineered for heavily urbanized zones. Built with structured chambers containing granular river gravel, graded sands, and bio-activated coconut shell charcoal to absorb chemicals, remove motor oil traces, and capture trash instantly.
