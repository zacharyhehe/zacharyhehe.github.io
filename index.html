Engineering & Ecosystems

Nature-Based Engineering Solutions

Discover three smart infrastructure tools that slow down and purify surface storm runoffs.

Biological Filtration

Rain Garden

Shallow planted basins designed with native vegetation to intercept urban surface water. These gardens act like natural sponges and filters, utilizing root systems and engineered soil substrates to trap heavy metals, pesticides, and hydrocarbons before they can access major marine buffers.

Riparian Buffers

Green Buffer

Robust bands of dense trees, shrubs, and native perennial grasses planted adjacent to streams and rivers. They create friction that slows down fast-moving storm water, allowing suspended sediment and particles to drop out of suspension while the plants absorb chemical runoff directly.

Urban Retrofit

Filter Box

Highly compact engineered structures deployed adjacent to street-level storm drains. Utilizing a system of packed gravel, course filter sand, and organic bio-charcoal, these boxes physically trap heavy pollutants and absorb organic odors inside tight metropolitan concrete spaces.
